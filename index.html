About This Project

This website is a student project for the course Western Classical Thought. It introduces Jean-Paul Sartre’s lecture Existentialism Is a Humanism through historical background, textual analysis, and key philosophical concepts.

↓

Why Sartre?

Sartre’s existentialism addresses fundamental questions of freedom, responsibility, and human dignity in a postwar context. His thought remains influential in philosophy, literature, and modern cultural theory.

↓

Site Structure

The site is organized into four main sections: an introduction to the author, the historical context of the work, an overview of the lecture itself, and an explanation of its key concepts. Each section is designed to guide the reader step by step.
